### Zero Liability Standard

**PROTECTED PARTIES SHALL HAVE ABSOLUTELY NO LIABILITY WHATSOEVER** for any damages, losses, injuries, harms, costs, or expenses of any kind, whether:

- Direct or Indirect
- Foreseeable or Unforeseeable
- Known or Unknown
- Present or Future
- Actual or Potential
- Compensatory or Punitive
- Economic or Non-Economic
- Tangible or Intangible

### Excluded Legal Theories

This liability exclusion applies to ALL legal theories, including but not limited to:

| Category | Excluded Theories |
|----------|-------------------|
| Tort | Negligence (ordinary, gross, criminal), strict liability, product liability |
| Contract | Breach of contract, breach of warranty, breach of implied terms |
| Professional | Medical malpractice, legal malpractice, professional negligence |
| Statutory | Violation of statutory duties, regulatory violations |
| Equitable | Unjust enrichment, promissory estoppel, constructive trust |
| Fiduciary | Breach of fiduciary duty, breach of loyalty |
| Fraud | Misrepresentation (negligent or fraudulent), fraud in the inducement |

## Layer 3: International Liability Shield

### Multi-Jurisdictional Protection

This liability shield applies in all jurisdictions worldwide. The following provisions address major regulatory frameworks:

#### European Union

- **Product Liability Directive:** Protected Parties are not manufacturers, importers, or suppliers of products
- **Medical Device Regulation (MDR):** No liability as repository contains research frameworks, not medical devices
- **GDPR:** No liability for user's data processing or controller/processor obligations
- **EU AI Act:** Research frameworks provided for professional oversight integration
- **Professional Liability:** No assumption of professional duties under EU member state laws
- **Consumer Protection:** Users are not consumers; materials provided for professional/research use only

#### United Kingdom

- **Consumer Rights Act:** No consumer contracts; all materials provided as-is for professional use
- **Medical Devices Regulations:** No liability as materials are research frameworks, not devices
- **MHRA Regulations:** No regulatory obligations or liabilities assumed
- **Professional Negligence:** No professional duties assumed under UK law
- **UK GDPR:** No data controller or processor obligations

#### United States

- **Federal Tort Claims:** Protected Parties are private entities with no federal liability
- **State Tort Law:** Maximum liability shields under all 50 states' laws
- **Product Liability (Restatement Third):** Not manufacturers, sellers, or distributors of products
- **Professional Malpractice:** No professional relationships established
- **HIPAA:** No covered entity or business associate obligations assumed
- **FDA:** No manufacturer or distributor obligations
- **State Licensing Laws:** No provision of licensed professional services

#### Canada

- **Provincial Tort Law:** No liability under tort law of any province or territory
- **Health Canada Regulations:** No medical device manufacturer or distributor obligations
- **PIPEDA and Provincial Privacy Laws:** No data custodian obligations
- **Professional Licensing:** No provision of licensed services in any province

#### Australia

- **Australian Consumer Law:** No consumer guarantees apply to research materials
- **TGA Regulations:** No therapeutic goods manufacturer or sponsor obligations
- **Privacy Act:** No Australian Privacy Principles obligations assumed
- **Professional Liability:** No professional services provided under Australian law

#### Asia-Pacific

- **Japan (PMDA):** No pharmaceutical or medical device obligations
- **China (NMPA):** No medical product registration or liability
- **Singapore (HSA):** No health products regulatory obligations
- **Individual Country Laws:** No liability under tort, professional, or regulatory laws of any jurisdiction
