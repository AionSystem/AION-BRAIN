## Legal Vocabulary Transmutation

### General to Legal-Specific Transformation
```
VOCABULARY ELEVATION EXAMPLES:

CONTRACTS:
├─ "agreement" → "contract" or "covenant" (depending on context)
├─ "promise" → "consideration" (if bargained-for exchange)
├─ "break a contract" → "breach" or "material breach"
├─ "end a contract" → "terminate," "rescind," "void," or "voidable"
├─ "both sides agree" → "mutual assent" or "meeting of the minds"
└─ Precision Gain: SUBSTANTIAL

TORTS:
├─ "careless" → "negligent"
├─ "on purpose" → "intentional" or "willful"
├─ "hurt" → "injury" or "damages"
├─ "fault" → "liability"
├─ "really careless" → "gross negligence" or "reckless"
└─ Precision Gain: VERY HIGH

CRIMINAL:
├─ "knew" → "mens rea" (specific intent level)
├─ "did it" → "actus reus"
├─ "guilty" → "convicted" (after trial) or "guilty plea"
├─ "innocent" → "not guilty" or "acquitted"
├─ "jail" → "incarceration" (general) or specific facility type
└─ Precision Gain: SUBSTANTIAL

PROCEDURE:
├─ "sue" → "file a complaint" or "commence an action"
├─ "ask the court" → "motion" or "petition"
├─ "disagree with ruling" → "appeal" (specific remedy sought)
├─ "prove" → "establish" (with burden specification)
├─ "evidence" → "admissible evidence" (if addressing admissibility)
└─ Precision Gain: HIGH

CONSTITUTIONAL:
├─ "right" → specific constitutional provision
├─ "freedom" → specific liberty interest
├─ "unfair" → "due process violation" or "equal protection violation"
├─ "government can't" → specific constitutional constraint
└─ Precision Gain: VERY HIGH
```

## Context-Specific Precision

### Jurisdiction-Aware Terminology
```
JURISDICTIONAL PRECISION:

FEDERAL vs. STATE:
├─ "trial court" → "district court" (federal) or "[state] trial court"
├─ "appeals court" → "circuit court" (federal) or "appellate court" (state)
├─ "supreme court" → "Supreme Court" (federal) or "[State] Supreme Court"
└─ Precision: Jurisdiction-specific terminology required

PRACTICE AREA:
├─ Corporate: "shareholders," "fiduciary duty," "piercing the veil"
├─ Family: "dissolution," "parenting time," "equitable distribution"
├─ Immigration: "alien," "LPR," "removal proceedings"
├─ Employment: "at-will," "protected class," "hostile work environment"
└─ Precision: Domain-specific vocabulary preferred

TEMPORAL:
├─ Pre-filing: "potential claim," "statute of limitations"
├─ Pleading: "complaint," "answer," "affirmative defense"
├─ Discovery: "interrogatories," "production," "deposition"
├─ Trial: "direct examination," "cross-examination," "verdict"
├─ Appeal: "notice of appeal," "brief," "oral argument"
└─ Precision: Stage-appropriate terminology
```

## Legal Precision Rating System

### Precision Assessment Framework
```
LEGAL PRECISION RATINGS:

MINIMAL:
├─ General synonym without legal significance
├─ Example: "agreement" → "deal"
├─ No precision gain
└─ Avoid in legal context

MODERATE:
├─ Narrows meaning somewhat
├─ Example: "agreement" → "arrangement"
├─ Limited precision gain
└─ Acceptable but not optimal

SUBSTANTIAL:
├─ Adds legal specificity
├─ Example: "agreement" → "contract"
├─ Meaningful precision gain
└─ Appropriate for most legal contexts

VERY HIGH:
├─ Combines specificity + perfect contextual fit
├─ Example: "agreement" → "bilateral executory contract"
├─ Maximum precision
└─ Appropriate for technical legal writing

RATING DERIVATION:
├─ Etymological analysis (root legal meaning)
├─ Legal dictionaries (Black's Law Dictionary)
├─ Case law usage (how courts use the term)
├─ Statutory definitions (if applicable)
└─ Contextual appropriateness (audience, purpose)
```

## Legal Writing Enhancement

### Style and Register
```
LEGAL REGISTER OPTIMIZATION:

FORMAL LEGAL WRITING:
├─ Use of terms of art
├─ Precision over accessibility
├─ Citation-heavy
├─ Example: "The defendant, having breached the implied 
│   covenant of good faith and fair dealing, is liable 
│   for consequential damages proximately caused thereby."
└─ Audience: Attorneys, judges

CLIENT COMMUNICATION:
├─ Plain language with technical accuracy
├─ Define legal terms
├─ Balance precision with clarity
├─ Example: "The other side broke their promise to deal 
│   fairly with you (what lawyers call 'bad faith'), and 
│   you can recover for the losses this caused."
└─ Audience: Clients, lay persons

COURT FILINGS:
├─ Highest precision required
├─ Proper form and format
├─ Technical compliance
├─ Example: "Plaintiff respectfully moves this Court for 
│   summary judgment pursuant to Fed. R. Civ. P. 56(a), 
│   on the grounds that no genuine dispute of material 
│   fact exists and Plaintiff is entitled to judgment 
│   as a matter of law."
└─ Audience: Court, opposing counsel
```
