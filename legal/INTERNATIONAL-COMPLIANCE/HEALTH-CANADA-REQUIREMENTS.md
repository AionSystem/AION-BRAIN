## Canadian Regulatory Framework

### Medical Devices Regulations (SOR/98-282)

Health Canada regulates medical devices under the Food and Drugs Act and Medical Devices Regulations.

### Classification System

| Class | Risk Level | Examples | Regulatory Pathway |
|-------|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Class I** | Lowest | Tongue depressors, bandages | Establishment License only |
| **Class II** | Low-moderate | Contact lenses, pregnancy tests | Medical Device License required |
| **Class III** | Moderate-high | Orthopedic implants, ventilators | MDL with quality systems audit |
| **Class IV** | Highest | Pacemakers, defibrillators | MDL with comprehensive review |

## Privacy Requirements

### Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)

| Requirement | Application |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Consent** | Obtain consent for health information |
| **Safeguards** | Implement appropriate security measures |
| **Retention** | Limit retention to necessary period |
| **Accuracy** | Keep health information accurate |

### Provincial Health Privacy Laws

| Province | Law | Key Requirements |
|----------|-----|------------------|
| Ontario | PHIPA | Health information custodian requirements |
| Alberta | HIA | Health information rules |
