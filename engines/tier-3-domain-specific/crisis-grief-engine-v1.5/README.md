# Crisis & Grief Counseling Engine v1.5

**Domain:** Suicide Prevention, Death Services, Grief Counseling, Crisis Intervention

**Status:** Production Ready

**Classification:** Tier 3 - Domain-Specific Engine

---

## Overview

The Crisis & Grief Counseling Engine is designed to support mental health professionals, crisis counselors, hospice workers, funeral directors, and grief therapists in high-stakes emotional support contexts. This engine enhances AI reasoning for sensitive conversations involving suicide prevention, end-of-life care, bereavement support, and crisis intervention.

**This engine does NOT replace professional crisis intervention.** It provides structured frameworks to help professionals deliver more consistent, evidence-based support.

---

## Critical Safety Notice

This engine operates in life-critical contexts. Mandatory safety features include:

- **Immediate Escalation Protocols** — Automatic detection of imminent risk
- **Professional Boundary Enforcement** — Clear scope limitations
- **Human Oversight Requirements** — No autonomous crisis decisions
- **Liability Protection Layers** — Documentation and audit trails
- **Cultural Sensitivity Modules** — Grief varies across cultures

---

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Crisis Assessment Support
- Risk level classification (low/moderate/high/imminent)
- Warning sign identification
- Protective factor recognition
- Safety planning assistance

### 2. Grief Processing Frameworks
- Stage-agnostic grief support (beyond Kubler-Ross)
- Complicated grief identification
- Meaning-making facilitation
- Continuing bonds support

### 3. Death Services Support
- Family notification guidance
- Funeral planning assistance
- Legal/administrative navigation
- Cultural/religious accommodation

### 4. Professional Support
- Compassion fatigue detection
- Secondary trauma awareness
- Self-care reminders
- Supervision prompts

## Safety Architecture

### Layer 1: Immediate Risk Detection
Automatic identification of:
- Active suicidal ideation with plan
- Homicidal ideation
- Imminent self-harm
- Welfare check triggers

### Layer 2: Professional Boundary Enforcement
- Scope limitation reminders
- Referral prompts
- Licensure requirements
- Duty-to-warn triggers

### Layer 3: Cultural Sensitivity
- Religious/spiritual accommodation
- Cultural grief expressions
- Language/communication preferences
- Family structure awareness

- Session documentation support
- Risk assessment records
- Intervention tracking
- Supervision notes
