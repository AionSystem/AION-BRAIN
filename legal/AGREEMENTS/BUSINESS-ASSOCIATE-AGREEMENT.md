### RECITALS

WHEREAS, Covered Entity is a "Covered Entity" as defined by the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA") and its implementing regulations;

WHEREAS, Business Associate provides services to Covered Entity that involve the use or disclosure of Protected Health Information;

WHEREAS, HIPAA and the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act ("HITECH Act") require Covered Entity to obtain satisfactory assurances that Business Associate will appropriately safeguard Protected Health Information;

WHEREAS, the parties wish to comply with HIPAA, the HITECH Act, and all applicable regulations;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

---

### ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

**1.1** Terms used but not defined in this Agreement shall have the meanings set forth in HIPAA, the HITECH Act, and implementing regulations at 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164.

**1.2 "Breach"** means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of Protected Health Information in a manner not permitted under the Privacy Rule which compromises the security or privacy of the PHI as defined in 45 CFR 164.402.

**1.3 "Business Associate"** means a person or entity that performs functions or activities on behalf of, or provides services to, a Covered Entity that involve the use or disclosure of PHI.

**1.4 "Covered Entity"** means a health plan, healthcare clearinghouse, or healthcare provider that transmits health information electronically in connection with a covered transaction.

**1.5 "Designated Record Set"** means a group of records maintained by or for a Covered Entity that includes medical and billing records, enrollment, payment, claims adjudication, and case or medical management record systems.

**1.6 "Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI"** means PHI that is transmitted or maintained in electronic media.

**1.7 "Individual"** means the person who is the subject of the PHI.

**1.8 "Privacy Rule"** means the Standards for Privacy of Individually Identifiable Health Information at 45 CFR Part 164, Subparts A and E.

**1.9 "Protected Health Information" or "PHI"** means individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium.

**1.10 "Required By Law"** means a mandate contained in law that compels an entity to use or disclose PHI.

**1.11 "Secretary"** means the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.

**1.12 "Security Incident"** means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system.

**1.13 "Security Rule"** means the Security Standards for the Protection of Electronic Protected Health Information at 45 CFR Part 164, Subparts A and C.

**1.14 "Subcontractor"** means a person to whom a Business Associate delegates a function, activity, or service, other than in the capacity of a workforce member.

**1.15 "Unsecured PHI"** means PHI that is not rendered unusable, unreadable, or indecipherable to unauthorized persons through the use of technology or methodology specified by the Secretary.

### ARTICLE 2: OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

**2.1 Permitted Uses and Disclosures.** Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only as permitted or required by this Agreement or as Required By Law. Specifically, Business Associate may:

(a) Use or disclose PHI to perform functions, activities, or services for Covered Entity as specified in the Underlying Agreement, provided that such use or disclosure would not violate the Privacy Rule if done by Covered Entity;

(b) Use or disclose PHI as Required By Law;

(c) Use PHI for the proper management and administration of Business Associate or to carry out its legal responsibilities;

(d) Disclose PHI for the proper management and administration of Business Associate if the disclosure is Required By Law, or Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the recipient that the PHI will be held confidentially, used or disclosed only as Required By Law or for the purpose disclosed, and the recipient will notify Business Associate of any breaches;

(e) Use PHI to provide Data Aggregation services to Covered Entity, if permitted under the Underlying Agreement;

(f) De-identify PHI in accordance with 45 CFR 164.514(a)-(c).

**2.2 Prohibited Uses and Disclosures.** Business Associate shall not:

(a) Use or disclose PHI other than as permitted or required by this Agreement or as Required By Law;

(b) Use or disclose PHI in a manner that would violate the Privacy Rule if done by Covered Entity;

(c) Use or disclose PHI for fundraising or marketing without prior written authorization;

(d) Sell PHI without prior written authorization from the Individual;

(e) Use genetic information for underwriting purposes.

**2.3 Safeguards.** Business Associate shall:

(a) Implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI;

(b) Comply with the Security Rule, including 45 CFR 164.308, 164.310, 164.312, and 164.316;

(c) Implement policies and procedures to prevent, detect, contain, and correct security violations;

(d) Conduct periodic risk analyses and implement security measures to reduce risks and vulnerabilities.

**2.4 Subcontractors.** Business Associate shall:

(a) Ensure that any Subcontractor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of Business Associate agrees to the same restrictions, conditions, and requirements that apply to Business Associate under this Agreement;

(b) Enter into a written agreement with each Subcontractor containing substantially similar terms as this Agreement;

(c) Remain liable for acts and omissions of Subcontractors.

**2.5 Reporting.** Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity:

(a) Any use or disclosure of PHI not permitted by this Agreement within `[NUMBER - typically 5-10]` business days of discovery;

(b) Any Security Incident of which it becomes aware within `[NUMBER - typically 24-72]` hours of discovery;

(c) Any Breach of Unsecured PHI within `[NUMBER - typically 24-48]` hours of discovery.

**2.6 Breach Notification.** In the event of a Breach of Unsecured PHI, Business Associate shall:

(a) Provide Covered Entity with sufficient information to enable notification to affected Individuals, the Secretary, and media (if applicable);

(b) Include in the notification: identification of affected Individuals, description of PHI involved, date of Breach, date of discovery, description of what happened and response actions;

(c) Cooperate with Covered Entity's investigation and mitigation efforts;

(d) Preserve evidence related to the Breach.

**2.7 Access to PHI.** Business Associate shall:

(a) Make PHI in a Designated Record Set available to Covered Entity or the Individual as required by 45 CFR 164.524;

(b) Respond to access requests within `[NUMBER]` days of receipt;

(c) Provide PHI in the form or format requested if readily producible.

**2.8 Amendment of PHI.** Business Associate shall:

(a) Make PHI available for amendment as required by 45 CFR 164.526;

(b) Incorporate amendments into affected records within `[NUMBER]` days of receipt.

**2.9 Accounting of Disclosures.** Business Associate shall:

(a) Make available information required for Covered Entity to provide an accounting of disclosures as required by 45 CFR 164.528;

(b) Document disclosures and related information for at least six (6) years;

(c) Respond to accounting requests within `[NUMBER]` days.

**2.10 Access for Government Agencies.** Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary for purposes of determining Covered Entity's compliance with HIPAA.

**2.11 Minimum Necessary Standard.** Business Associate shall:

(a) Request, use, and disclose only the minimum necessary PHI to accomplish the intended purpose;

(b) Develop policies and procedures to identify persons needing access and limit access accordingly.

### ARTICLE 3: OBLIGATIONS OF COVERED ENTITY

**3.1 Notice of Privacy Practices.** Covered Entity shall provide Business Associate with its Notice of Privacy Practices and any changes thereto that affect Business Associate's permitted uses and disclosures.

**3.2 Restrictions and Permissions.** Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of:

(a) Any restrictions on use or disclosure of PHI agreed to with Individuals;

(b) Any changes in, or revocation of, authorizations by Individuals;

(c) Any other relevant restrictions that may affect Business Associate's obligations.

**3.3 Impermissible Requests.** Covered Entity shall not request Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner that would violate HIPAA if done by Covered Entity.

---

### ARTICLE 4: TERM AND TERMINATION

**4.1 Term.** This Agreement shall be effective as of the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until terminated as provided herein.

**4.2 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement upon `[NUMBER]` days' written notice if the other party has materially breached this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within that period.

**4.3 Immediate Termination.** Covered Entity may immediately terminate this Agreement if:

(a) Business Associate has breached a material term and cure is not possible;

(b) Business Associate has knowingly violated a material term;

(c) Required by law.

**4.4 Obligations Upon Termination.** Upon termination, Business Associate shall:

(a) Return or destroy all PHI received from or created on behalf of Covered Entity;

(b) Retain no copies of PHI;

(c) If return or destruction is infeasible, extend the protections of this Agreement to any retained PHI and limit further uses and disclosures.

**4.5 Survival.** The obligations of Business Associate under Section 4.4 shall survive termination of this Agreement.

### ARTICLE 6: INDEMNIFICATION

**6.1 Business Associate Indemnification.** Business Associate shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Covered Entity from and against any claims, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from:

(a) Business Associate's breach of this Agreement;

(b) Business Associate's violation of HIPAA;

(c) Any Breach of Unsecured PHI caused by Business Associate.

**6.2 Covered Entity Indemnification.** Covered Entity shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Business Associate from and against any claims, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from:

(a) Covered Entity's breach of this Agreement;

(b) Covered Entity's violation of HIPAA;

(c) Any Breach of Unsecured PHI caused by Covered Entity.

**6.3 Limitations.** Indemnification obligations are subject to any limitations set forth in the Underlying Agreement.
