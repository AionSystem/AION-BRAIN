## EXAMPLE 1: CAREER DECISION â€” QUIT JOB TO START BUSINESS

### User Query
"Should I quit my stable $120K job as a software engineer to start my own SaaS company?"

### STANDARD MODE ANALYSIS

#### Step 1: Decision Frame Clarification

**Surface Question:** Should I quit my job to start a business?

**Underlying Question:** "Am I ready to trade predictable income and career trajectory for autonomy, potential upside, and significant risk?"

**Decision Variables:**
- Timing of departure (now vs. 6 months vs. 1 year)
- Full quit vs. side project first
- Target business model and market

**Success Criteria:**
- Minimum: Cover living expenses within 18 months
- Target: Replace current income within 24 months
- Stretch: $500K ARR within 3 years

---

#### Step 2: Bias Detection (Kahneman)

**Detected Biases:**

| Bias | Severity | Evidence |
|------|----------|----------|
| Loss Aversion | MODERATE | Framing "stable" job as safe haven; overweighting what's given up |
| Availability | MILD | Recent friend's startup success story dominating thinking |
| Overconfidence | MODERATE | "I'm a good engineer, so I'll be a good founder" |
| Planning Fallacy | SEVERE | Underestimating time to product-market fit |

**Pre-Mortem Analysis:**

*Imagine it's 18 months later and your startup failed. Why?*

| Failure Cause | Probability | Severity |
|---------------|-------------|----------|
| Runway depleted before product-market fit | 40% | CRITICAL |
| Underestimated competition/market | 35% | HIGH |
| Burnout from solo founder stress | 25% | HIGH |
| Partner resentment destroyed relationship | 20% | CRITICAL |
| Technical debt from rushing MVP | 30% | MEDIUM |

**Mitigation Plans:**

1. **Runway risk:** Extend from 12 to 24 months OR reduce burn 40% by keeping apartment small
2. **Competition risk:** Deep customer discovery before building; validate 50 conversations minimum
3. **Partner resentment:** Weekly check-ins, explicit support contract, shared success definition

**Debiasing Applied:**
- Loss aversion â†’ Reframe: "What do I GAIN by starting now vs. waiting 5 more years?"
- Planning fallacy â†’ Reference class: "Most B2B SaaS take 2-3 years to hit $100K ARR"

---

#### Step 3: Satisficing (Simon)

**Aspiration Levels:**

| Level | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| MINIMUM | Cover living expenses ($4K/month) within 18 months |
| TARGET | Replace salary ($10K/month) within 24 months |
| STRETCH | $500K ARR, hire first employee within 36 months |
| UNACCEPTABLE | Deplete savings below 3-month emergency fund |

**Decision Deadline:**

Reversibility assessment: MEDIUM (Can return to employment, but 2+ year gap impacts trajectory)

Recommended deadline: **60 days** to make final decision with more data:
- Complete 30 customer discovery interviews
- Validate MVP concept with landing page
- Get partner's explicit written support

**Search Stopping Rule:** If 30 interviews confirm problem AND partner explicitly supports AND runway confirmed at 24 months â†’ PROCEED

---

#### Step 4: Optionality Analysis (Taleb)

**Fragility Classification:** FRAGILE â†’ ROBUST (with modifications)

Current plan is FRAGILE because:
- All-in quit with 12-month runway
- Single product bet
- Solo founder (single point of failure)

Redesigned to ROBUST:
- Extend runway to 24 months
- Build audience first (newsletter/Twitter) before quitting
- Find co-founder or advisor network

**Optionality Assessment:**

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| Maximum Loss | 24 months salary + career momentum = ~$240K |
| Is Loss Capped? | YES (worst case: return to employment at lower level) |
| Maximum Gain | Unlimited (successful exit could be $1M-$100M+) |
| Is Gain Unlimited? | YES |
| Reversibility | MEDIUM (can return, but 2-year gap) |

**Optionality Score: HIGH** (capped downside, unlimited upside, partially reversible)

**Barbell Strategy:**

| Conservative (90%) | Aggressive (10%) |
|--------------------|------------------|
| 24-month runway secured | SaaS bet |
| Health insurance via COBRA/ACA | Full founder commitment |
| Partner relationship protected | All energy into startup |
| Return option maintained | Geographic flexibility |

**Black Swan Scenarios:**

| Scenario | Impact | Mitigation |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Recession 6 months in | Harder fundraising, slower sales | Bootstrap-focused, extend runway |
| Partner leaves | Emotional devastation, financial stress | Monthly relationship check-ins |
| Health crisis | Depletes runway, halts progress | Health insurance non-negotiable |
| Better opportunity appears | Regret, FOMO | Define what would make you pivot |

**Via Negativa (What to Remove):**
1. Remove: Expensive apartment (reduce burn 30%)
2. Remove: Pressure to raise VC immediately (bootstrap first)
3. Remove: Assumption that you must build product first (audience first)

---

#### Step 5: Temporal Assessment (Bergson)

**Current Phase:** RIPENESS

Evidence:
- 7 years software experience (skills ready)
- $180K savings (financial runway exists)
- No children yet (personal flexibility)
- Market timing for SaaS tools favorable
- Age 32 (energy + experience balanced)

**External Alignment: HIGH**
- SaaS market growing

- Lower barrier to start than ever

**Internal Alignment: MEDIUM-HIGH**

**Temporal Decision Matrix:**

- Verdict: **ACT NOW** (with 60-day preparation period)

### User Query
"I've been with my partner for 4 years. We're both 34. I'm not sure if I should propose or end things."

| Directional | Seeking permission to leave? Or seeking validation to stay? Unclear |
| Emotional | Anxiety-dominant (uncertainty, pressure, not joy or love) |
| Risk | Binary thinking may force premature closure |

**Reframed Question:**

Original: "Should I propose or end things?"

#### Step 2: Bias Detection (Kahneman)

**Detected Biases:**

| Bias | Severity | Evidence |
|------|----------|----------|
| Sunk Cost | SEVERE | "4 years invested" weighs heavily |
| Status Quo | MODERATE | Easier to continue than change |
| Social Proof | MODERATE | Friends married, creates pressure |
| Loss Aversion | SEVERE | Fear of being alone at 34+ |

**Pre-Mortem (Marry Scenario):**

*It's 3 years later, and the marriage failed. Why?*

**Pre-Mortem (End Scenario):**

*It's 3 years later, and you regret ending it. Why?*

2. Undervalued partner's qualities (P: 25%, Severity: MEDIUM)
3. Loneliness worse than expected (P: 35%, Severity: MEDIUM)

#### Step 4: Optionality Analysis (Taleb)

**Reversibility Assessment:**

| Delay decision 6-12 months | HIGH (fully reversible) |

**Taleb's Insight:** When faced with an irreversible decision under uncertainty, **delay is an option**.

**Via Negativa:** What can be removed?
1. Remove: Artificial timeline pressure
2. Remove: Binary framing (propose vs. end)
3. Remove: Assumption that doubt = wrong relationship

**Antifragility Test:**

Evidence needed:
- How did you handle COVID together?
- How do you navigate conflict?
- What happened during career setbacks?

- **Emergence:** Relationship growing, just needs commitment catalyst
- **Decay:** Relationship fading, staying due to inertia

**Diagnostic Questions:**
1. Do you look forward to seeing them? Or is it obligation?
2. Are you growing together or growing apart?
3. Is the doubt about THEM or about MARRIAGE ITSELF?
4. Would you choose them again today, knowing everything?

**Temporal Verdict:** NEED MORE DATA

---

**Stakeholder Analysis:**

Least advantaged: **Partner** (has less information about your doubts)

**Veil of Ignorance Test:**

Answer: **YES** â€” They deserve full information to make their own choice.

**Ethical Imperative:** Have honest conversation BEFORE any decision.

## VALIDATION: CONFIDENCE CALIBRATION

### How Confidence Levels Work

| Level | Criteria | Action Implication |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| **HIGH** | 4-5 frameworks converge independently | Proceed with recommendation |
| **MEDIUM** | 2-3 frameworks converge | Proceed with caution, gather more data |
| **LOW** | Single framework or contradictions | Delay, seek external input |
| **VERY LOW** | Insufficient data, frameworks conflict | Do not proceed until resolved |

### External Validation Triggers

Recommend seeking external expert when:
- Decision involves technical expertise (medical, legal, financial)
- Confidence remains LOW after full analysis
- Stakes are irreversible AND high
- Frameworks remain in unresolved conflict
