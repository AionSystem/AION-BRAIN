## Medical Domain Professional Boundaries

### Mandatory Licensed Physician Oversight

**ALL MEDICAL/CLINICAL APPLICATIONS ABSOLUTELY REQUIRE:**

#### Licensed Physician Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Medical License | Active MD or DO license in good standing |
| Board Certification | Relevant specialty (preferred) |
| Malpractice Insurance | $1M/$3M minimum coverage |
| Practice Setting | Hospital privileges or appropriate setting |
| DEA Registration | If prescribing controlled substances |
| State Licenses | All jurisdictions of practice |
| Disciplinary History | No suspension, revocation, or discipline |

#### Scope of Practice Requirements

**PHYSICIANS MUST:**

- Make all final clinical decisions and diagnoses
- Review and approve all treatment recommendations
- Verify all medication dosing and prescriptions
- Assess all patient safety and risk factors
- Obtain informed consent from patients
- Document all clinical reasoning and decisions
- Maintain medical records in compliance with regulations
- Report adverse events and medical errors
- Supervise all non-physician clinical staff
- Comply with all HIPAA and PHI protection requirements
- Follow all FDA regulations and medical device requirements
- Adhere to all state medical board regulations
- Maintain professional liability insurance coverage

**PHYSICIANS MAY NOT:**

- Rely solely on AI outputs for clinical decisions
- Delegate clinical judgment to automated systems
- Use unvalidated AI tools for patient care
- Practice outside their scope of training and competence
- Violate patient confidentiality or HIPAA
- Fail to obtain informed consent
- Practice medicine without valid license
- Prescribe without proper DEA registration
- Fail to report adverse events
- Abandon patients or fail to provide continuity of care

### Professional Standards Compliance

#### American Medical Association (AMA) Code of Medical Ethics

- Respect patient autonomy and informed consent
- Maintain patient confidentiality and privacy
- Act in patient's best interests (beneficence)
- Do no harm (non-maleficence)
- Promote justice and equitable healthcare access
- Maintain professional competence through continuing education
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Be honest and transparent with patients

#### Hippocratic Oath Principles

- First, do no harm
- Respect patient privacy
- Maintain professional boundaries
- Practice medicine for patient benefit
- Never take advantage of patient trust

### Regulatory Compliance

#### Federal Regulations

| Agency | Requirements |
|--------|--------------|
| FDA | Medical device regulations, clinical decision support software classification |
| HHS/OCR | HIPAA Privacy Rule, Security Rule, Breach Notification Rule |
| CMS | Medicare/Medicaid conditions of participation |
| DEA | Controlled substance prescribing regulations |
| CLIA | Clinical laboratory improvement amendments |
| OIG | Stark Law, Anti-Kickback Statute compliance |

#### State Regulations

- State medical practice acts
- State medical board regulations
- State telemedicine laws
- State prescribing regulations
- State privacy laws (beyond HIPAA)
- State controlled substance laws
- State medical records laws

#### Accreditation Standards

- **Joint Commission:** Hospital and ambulatory care accreditation
- **NCQA:** Health plan and medical group accreditation
- **AAAHC:** Ambulatory healthcare accreditation
- **URAC:** Healthcare quality and accreditation

### Specialty-Specific Requirements

| Specialty | Additional Requirements |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Cardiology | Board certification, ACLS, echocardiography credentials |
| Oncology | Board certification, chemotherapy administration certification |
| Pediatrics | Board certification, PALS, child abuse reporting knowledge |
| Emergency Medicine | Board certification, ACLS, PALS, ATLS, EMTALA compliance |
| Psychiatry | Board certification, suicidal assessment competency, commitment law knowledge |

### Continuing Medical Education (CME)

**REQUIRED CME:**

- Minimum 50 hours annually (varies by state and specialty)
- Medical ethics (required)
- Risk management and patient safety
- Cultural competency
- Opioid prescribing (required in many states)
- Infection control
- HIPAA and privacy compliance
- Specialty-specific updates

### Professional Liability Insurance

**MINIMUM COVERAGE:**

| Type | Coverage |
|------|----------|
| Occurrence-based | $1M per occurrence / $3M aggregate |
| Claims-made | $1M per claim / $3M aggregate + tail coverage |
| Cyber liability | HIPAA/PHI breach coverage |
| Scope | All practice locations and telemedicine |

### Prohibited Medical Activities Without Physician Oversight

**ABSOLUTELY PROHIBITED WITHOUT LICENSED PHYSICIAN:**

- Patient diagnosis or differential diagnosis
- Treatment recommendations or care plans
- Medication prescribing or dosing
- Medical device operation or control
- Surgery planning or intraoperative guidance
- Emergency medicine or triage decisions
- Mental health diagnosis or treatment
- Pregnancy or pediatric care decisions
- End-of-life or palliative care decisions
- Medical imaging interpretation (radiology, pathology)
- Laboratory result interpretation
- Clinical trial enrollment or protocol design
- Genetic counseling or genomic interpretation
- Telemedicine consultation
- Controlled substance prescribing

## Legal Domain Professional Boundaries

### Mandatory Licensed Attorney Supervision

**ALL LEGAL APPLICATIONS ABSOLUTELY REQUIRE:**

#### Licensed Attorney Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Bar Admission | Active admission in good standing in relevant jurisdiction(s) |
| Malpractice Insurance | $1M minimum coverage |
| Disciplinary History | No bar discipline or ethics violations |
| Specialized Expertise | Relevant practice area (preferred) |
| Trust Account | If handling client funds |
| CLE Compliance | Current continuing legal education |

#### Scope of Practice Requirements

**ATTORNEYS MUST:**

- Provide all legal advice and counsel to clients
- Review and approve all legal documents and filings
- Verify all legal citations and precedents
- Assess all legal strategy and risks
- Maintain attorney-client confidentiality and privilege
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Comply with all rules of professional conduct
- Supervise all non-attorney legal staff
- Meet all court deadlines and procedural requirements
- Represent clients competently and diligently
- Communicate with clients regularly and transparently
- Maintain client trust accounts in compliance with rules

**ATTORNEYS MAY NOT:**

- Rely solely on AI outputs for legal advice
- Delegate professional judgment to automated systems
- Use unvalidated AI tools for client representation
- Practice outside their jurisdictional bar admission
- Practice in areas where not competent
- Breach attorney-client privilege or confidentiality
- Fail to avoid conflicts of interest
- Miss court deadlines or filing requirements
- Misappropriate client funds
- Make frivolous arguments or file frivolous lawsuits
- Engage in unauthorized practice of law

### Professional Standards Compliance

#### ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Rule 1.1 | Competence - must provide competent representation |
| Rule 1.3 | Diligence - must act with reasonable diligence |
| Rule 1.4 | Communication - must keep client informed |
| Rule 1.5 | Fees - must charge reasonable fees |
| Rule 1.6 | Confidentiality - must maintain client confidences |
| Rule 1.7-1.9 | Conflicts of Interest - must avoid conflicts |
| Rule 1.15 | Safekeeping Property - must properly handle client funds |
| Rule 3.1 | Meritorious Claims - no frivolous claims |
| Rule 3.3 | Candor to Tribunal - must be truthful to court |
| Rule 4.1 | Truthfulness to Others - no false statements |
| Rule 5.3 | Supervision of Nonlawyer Assistants - must supervise staff |
| Rule 5.5 | Unauthorized Practice of Law - no UPL |
| Rule 8.4 | Misconduct - professional conduct required |

- State bar admission requirements
- State CLE requirements
- State client trust account rules
- State fee arbitration rules
- State attorney advertising rules
- State multijurisdictional practice rules

#### Federal Regulations

- Federal court admission requirements

- Federal Rules of Evidence compliance

### Practice Area-Specific Requirements

| Practice Area | Specific Requirements |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Litigation | Trial experience, discovery management, motion practice |
| Corporate/Transactional | Contract drafting, M&A experience, securities compliance |
| Family Law | Divorce/custody knowledge, mediation training |

### Continuing Legal Education (CLE)

**REQUIRED CLE (varies by state):**

- Minimum 12-15 hours annually
- Legal ethics (required, typically 1-4 hours)
- Substance abuse awareness (required in some states)
- Bias elimination (required in some states)
- Technology and cybersecurity
- Practice area-specific updates

### Professional Liability Insurance

**MINIMUM COVERAGE:**

| Type | Coverage |
|------|----------|
| Claims-made | $1M per claim / $3M aggregate |
| Tail coverage | Required when changing policies |
| Scope | All practice areas and jurisdictions |
| Cyber liability | Data breach coverage |
| Trust account | Dishonesty coverage |

### Prohibited Legal Activities Without Attorney Supervision

**ABSOLUTELY PROHIBITED WITHOUT LICENSED ATTORNEY:**

- Providing legal advice to clients
- Drafting legal documents (contracts, wills, pleadings)
- Representing parties in legal proceedings
- Negotiating legal agreements
- Legal research as sole basis for legal positions
- Legal strategy or case management
- Client intake and engagement
- Fee setting and billing
- Trust account management
- Court filings and appearances
- Discovery management
- Settlement negotiations
- Immigration applications or petitions
- Patent or trademark filings

## Scientific Domain Professional Boundaries

### Mandatory Scientific Professional Supervision

**ALL SCIENTIFIC/RESEARCH APPLICATIONS ABSOLUTELY REQUIRE:**

#### Qualified Researcher Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Credentials | PhD or equivalent in relevant scientific discipline |
| Position | Active research position at accredited institution |
| Ethics Training | CITI or equivalent research ethics certification |
| IRB Approval | Institutional review board approval for human subjects |
| IACUC Approval | Approval for animal research |
| Funding Compliance | Adherence to funding agency requirements |

#### Scope of Practice Requirements

**RESEARCHERS MUST:**

- Design scientifically rigorous research methodologies
- Ensure statistical validity and power
- Obtain IRB approval for human subjects research
- Obtain IACUC approval for animal research
- Maintain data integrity and reproducibility
- Follow authorship and publication ethics
- Comply with funding agency requirements
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Report research misconduct
- Maintain laboratory safety standards
- Protect intellectual property appropriately
- Ensure research subject safety and informed consent

**RESEARCHERS MAY NOT:**

- Fabricate or falsify data
- Plagiarize others' work
- Conduct human subjects research without IRB approval
- Conduct animal research without IACUC approval
- Violate research subject confidentiality
- Fail to disclose conflicts of interest
- Misuse research funds
- Engage in authorship disputes or ghost authorship
- Fail to report research misconduct
- Violate intellectual property rights

### Professional Standards Compliance

#### NIH Responsible Conduct of Research

- Data acquisition, management, sharing, and ownership
- Conflict of interest and commitment
- Human subjects protection
- Animal welfare
- Research misconduct
- Publication practices and authorship
- Mentor/trainee responsibilities
- Peer review
- Collaborative science

#### Singapore Statement on Research Integrity

- Honesty in all aspects of research
- Accountability in research conduct
- Professional courtesy and fairness
- Good stewardship of research resources

### Regulatory Compliance

#### Institutional Requirements

| Committee | Scope |
|-----------|-------|
| IRB | Human subjects research approval |
| IACUC | Animal research approval |
| IBC | Biohazard research approval |
| COI Committee | Conflict of interest disclosure and management |
| Export Control | ITAR/EAR compliance |

#### Federal Regulations

| Regulation | Application |
|------------|-------------|
| Common Rule (45 CFR 46) | Human subjects research protections |
| HIPAA | If using protected health information |
| Animal Welfare Act | Animal research standards |
| NIH Guidelines | Recombinant DNA research |
| FDA | Clinical trial regulations |
| OSHA | Laboratory safety standards |

### Prohibited Research Activities Without Qualified Oversight

**ABSOLUTELY PROHIBITED WITHOUT QUALIFIED RESEARCHER:**

- Human subjects research without IRB approval
- Animal research without IACUC approval
- Clinical trial conduct without proper approvals
- Research involving biohazards without IBC approval
- Research with vulnerable populations without special protections
- Genetic modification research without appropriate approvals
- Research involving controlled substances without DEA registration
- Export-controlled research without proper authorization
- Publication without proper authorship attribution

## Financial Domain Professional Boundaries

### Mandatory Financial Professional Supervision

**ALL FINANCIAL/INVESTMENT APPLICATIONS ABSOLUTELY REQUIRE:**

#### Licensed Financial Professional Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Certification | CFP, CFA, CPA, or equivalent |
| Securities Licenses | Series 7 and Series 65/66 (for securities) |
| Registration | SEC or state regulator registration |
| Insurance | Professional liability (E&O) coverage |
| Background | FINRA background check clearance |
| Disciplinary History | No regulatory violations |

#### Scope of Practice Requirements

**FINANCIAL PROFESSIONALS MUST:**

- Assess client financial situation and suitability
- Provide investment advice in client's best interest (Reg BI)
- Disclose all fees, commissions, and conflicts of interest
- Comply with fiduciary duties (if applicable)
- Maintain client confidentiality
- Follow anti-money laundering (AML) procedures
- Complete suitability and know-your-customer (KYC) requirements
- Provide required disclosures (Form ADV, Form CRS, etc.)
- Document all investment recommendations and rationale
- Comply with all SEC and FINRA regulations
- Maintain professional liability insurance
- Report suspicious activity (SAR filings)

**FINANCIAL PROFESSIONALS MAY NOT:**

- Provide unsuitable investment recommendations
- Fail to disclose conflicts of interest
- Charge excessive or hidden fees
- Engage in fraudulent or deceptive practices
- Front-run client trades
- Misappropriate client funds
- Provide investment advice without proper registration
- Violate insider trading laws
- Fail to follow AML/KYC procedures
- Make guarantees of investment performance

### Professional Standards Compliance

#### CFP Board Code of Ethics

- Act with integrity, competence, and diligence
- Put client interests first (fiduciary duty)
- Provide full and fair disclosure
- Avoid or manage conflicts of interest
- Maintain client confidentiality
- Act with professionalism

#### CFA Institute Code and Standards

- Professionalism (knowledge of law, independence, misrepresentation, misconduct)
- Integrity of capital markets (material nonpublic information, market manipulation)
- Duties to clients (loyalty, fair dealing, suitability, performance presentation, confidentiality)
- Duties to employers
- Investment analysis and recommendations
- Conflicts of interest

### Regulatory Compliance

#### Federal Securities Regulations

| Regulation | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| Reg BI | Act in client's best interest |
| Investment Advisers Act of 1940 | RIA registration and fiduciary duty |
| Securities Act of 1933 | Securities registration and disclosure |
| Securities Exchange Act of 1934 | Trading regulations, insider trading prohibitions |
| FINRA Rules | Suitability, know-your-customer, supervision |
| Dodd-Frank Act | Fiduciary duty, whistleblower protections |
| Bank Secrecy Act | AML requirements |

### Prohibited Financial Activities Without Licensed Professional

**ABSOLUTELY PROHIBITED WITHOUT LICENSED FINANCIAL PROFESSIONAL:**

- Providing investment advice for compensation
- Securities trading or portfolio management
- Tax advice or return preparation
- Estate planning or wealth transfer advice
- Insurance product sales or recommendations
- Retirement planning or pension advice
- Mortgage or lending advice
- Credit counseling or debt management
- Financial planning or wealth management
- Investment performance claims or guarantees

### Mandatory Security Professional Supervision

**ALL CYBERSECURITY/SECURITY APPLICATIONS ABSOLUTELY REQUIRE:**

#### Certified Security Professional Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Certification | CISSP, CISM, CEH, or equivalent |
| Experience | Relevant security experience |
| Background | Security clearance if applicable |
| Insurance | Professional liability and cyber coverage |
| Ethics | Adherence to professional codes of conduct |

#### Scope of Practice Requirements

**SECURITY PROFESSIONALS MUST:**

- Conduct authorized security assessments only
- Maintain confidentiality of security findings
- Follow responsible disclosure practices
- Comply with applicable laws and regulations
- Document all security testing and findings
- Provide accurate risk assessments
- Follow organizational security policies
- Report security incidents appropriately
- Maintain professional certifications

**SECURITY PROFESSIONALS MAY NOT:**

- Conduct unauthorized penetration testing
- Access systems without proper authorization
- Disclose vulnerabilities irresponsibly
- Misrepresent security qualifications
- Engage in malicious hacking activities
- Violate privacy or data protection laws
- Fail to report critical vulnerabilities
- Misuse security tools or access

- Protect society, the common good, necessary public trust and confidence, and the infrastructure
- Act honorably, honestly, justly, responsibly, and legally
- Provide diligent and competent service to principals
- Advance and protect the profession

### Prohibited Security Activities Without Qualified Professional

**ABSOLUTELY PROHIBITED WITHOUT CERTIFIED SECURITY PROFESSIONAL:**

- Penetration testing or vulnerability assessments
- Security architecture design for critical systems
- Incident response for active breaches
- Forensic investigation and evidence handling
- Security policy development for regulated industries
- Compliance assessments for regulatory frameworks
- Red team exercises or adversarial simulations

### Professional Handoff Protocols

When crossing professional boundaries:

1. **Identify** all applicable professional domains using the oversight matrix
2. **Engage** licensed professionals in each required domain
3. **Document** professional oversight chain with clear accountability
4. **Maintain** clear role boundaries and escalation procedures
5. **Ensure** appropriate insurance coverage for all domains
6. **Coordinate** inter-professional communication protocols
7. **Establish** conflict resolution procedures between domains
