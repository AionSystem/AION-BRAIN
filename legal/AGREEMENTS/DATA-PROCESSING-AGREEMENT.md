### RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Controller has engaged the Processor to provide certain services that involve the processing of personal data;

WHEREAS, the parties wish to ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR") and other applicable privacy legislation;

WHEREAS, Article 28 of the GDPR requires that processing by a processor be governed by a contract setting out the subject matter, duration, nature, and purpose of processing;

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

---

### ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

**1.1** Unless otherwise defined herein, terms shall have the meanings set forth in applicable Data Protection Laws.

**1.2 "Applicable Data Protection Laws"** means all laws and regulations relating to the processing of personal data applicable to the processing under this DPA, including:

(a) GDPR (EU General Data Protection Regulation)

(b) UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018

(c) CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act/California Privacy Rights Act)

(d) LGPD (Brazil General Data Protection Law)

(e) Other applicable national and regional privacy laws

**1.3 "Controller"** means the natural or legal person which determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data.

**1.4 "Data Subject"** means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is processed.

**1.5 "Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

**1.6 "Personal Data Breach"** means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data.

**1.7 "Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means.

**1.8 "Processor"** means the natural or legal person which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

**1.9 "Special Categories of Personal Data"** means Personal Data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data, health data, or data concerning sex life or sexual orientation.

**1.10 "Sub-processor"** means any third party engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

### ARTICLE 3: PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

**3.1 Compliance.** The Processor shall:

(a) Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller

(b) Ensure persons authorized to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations

(c) Take all required security measures

(d) Respect conditions for engaging Sub-processors

(e) Assist the Controller with Data Subject rights requests

(f) Assist the Controller with GDPR compliance obligations

(g) Delete or return Personal Data after processing ends

(h) Provide information to demonstrate compliance

**3.2 Instructions.** The Processor shall:

(a) Process Personal Data only for the purposes described in Annex 1

(b) Immediately inform the Controller if an instruction infringes Applicable Data Protection Laws

(c) Not process Personal Data for any other purpose without Controller authorization

**3.3 Confidentiality.** The Processor shall ensure that:

(a) All personnel processing Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations

(b) Confidentiality obligations survive termination of employment

(c) Access to Personal Data is limited to personnel with a need to know

**3.4 Security Measures.** The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures as described in Annex 2 (Security Measures), including:

(a) Pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data

(b) Ability to ensure confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience

(c) Ability to restore availability and access in case of incident

(d) Process for regularly testing and evaluating security measures

### ARTICLE 5: DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

**5.1 Assistance.** The Processor shall assist the Controller in responding to Data Subject requests, including:

(a) Access requests

(b) Rectification requests

(c) Erasure requests ("right to be forgotten")

(d) Restriction of processing requests

(e) Data portability requests

(f) Objection to processing

**5.2 Notification.** If the Processor receives a Data Subject request directly:

(a) It shall promptly notify the Controller

(b) It shall not respond directly unless authorized by the Controller

(c) It shall provide reasonable cooperation to the Controller

**5.3 Response Timeframes.** The Processor shall assist the Controller in meeting statutory response deadlines.

---

### ARTICLE 6: PERSONAL DATA BREACH

**6.1 Notification to Controller.** Upon becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach, the Processor shall:

(a) Notify the Controller without undue delay, and in any event within `[NUMBER - typically 24-48]` hours

(b) Provide sufficient information for the Controller to meet its notification obligations

**6.2 Notification Content.** Breach notifications shall include:

(a) Description of the nature of the breach

(b) Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected

(c) Categories and approximate number of Personal Data records affected

(d) Name and contact details of the Processor's contact point

(e) Description of likely consequences

(f) Description of measures taken or proposed to address the breach

**6.3 Cooperation.** The Processor shall:

(a) Cooperate with the Controller's investigation

(b) Take immediate steps to mitigate the breach

(c) Preserve evidence related to the breach

(d) Assist with notifications to supervisory authorities and Data Subjects

**6.4 Documentation.** The Processor shall maintain records of all Personal Data Breaches.

---

### ARTICLE 7: DATA PROTECTION IMPACT ASSESSMENTS

**7.1 Assistance.** The Processor shall assist the Controller with:

(a) Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)

(b) Prior consultations with supervisory authorities

**7.2 Information.** The Processor shall provide:

(a) Information about processing operations

(b) Security measures implemented

(c) Risk assessments

(d) Other information reasonably requested by the Controller

---

### ARTICLE 8: INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

**8.1 Transfer Restrictions.** Personal Data shall not be transferred outside the EEA (or UK, as applicable) unless:

(a) The destination country has an adequacy decision

(b) Appropriate safeguards are in place (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses)

(c) A derogation applies under Article 49 GDPR

**8.2 Standard Contractual Clauses.** If transfers require Standard Contractual Clauses:

(a) The parties agree to execute the EU Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (Module Two: Controller to Processor)

(b) The SCCs are incorporated by reference as Annex 4

(c) The parties shall complete all required appendices

**8.3 UK International Data Transfer Agreement.** For UK transfers:

(a) The parties shall execute the UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or UK Addendum to the EU SCCs

(b) Such agreement is incorporated by reference as Annex 5

**8.4 Transfer Impact Assessment.** Where required:

(a) The Processor shall cooperate with transfer impact assessments

(b) The Processor shall provide information about destination country laws

(c) The parties shall implement supplementary measures as needed

### ARTICLE 9: AUDITS AND INSPECTIONS

**9.1 Audit Rights.** The Controller may:

(a) Audit the Processor's compliance with this DPA

(b) Inspect facilities where Personal Data is processed

(c) Require the Processor to provide compliance certifications

**9.2 Audit Process.** Audits shall be conducted:

(a) Upon reasonable advance notice (minimum `[NUMBER]` business days)

(b) During normal business hours

(c) Subject to reasonable confidentiality protections

(d) At the Controller's expense (unless audit reveals material non-compliance)

**9.3 Third-Party Audits.** The Controller may use third-party auditors subject to:

(a) Confidentiality agreements

(b) Exclusion of the Processor's competitors

**9.4 Alternative Evidence.** The Processor may satisfy audit requirements by providing:

(a) Current third-party certifications (e.g., ISO 27001, SOC 2)

(b) Summary audit reports

(c) Other evidence reasonably satisfactory to the Controller

### ARTICLE 10: DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

**10.1 Retention Period.** Personal Data shall be retained only for the duration specified in Annex 1, unless otherwise required by law.

**10.2 Upon Termination.** Upon termination of this DPA or the Principal Agreement:

(a) The Processor shall delete or return all Personal Data, at the Controller's choice

(b) Deletion shall occur within `[NUMBER]` days

(c) The Processor shall certify deletion in writing

**10.3 Exceptions.** The Processor may retain Personal Data if:

(a) Required by applicable law

(b) Stored in routine backup systems (subject to deletion upon restoration)

(c) Required for legal claims (limited retention with security measures)

### ARTICLE 11: LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

**11.1 Liability Allocation.** Each party shall be liable for damages caused by its breach of Applicable Data Protection Laws.

**11.2 Indemnification.** Each party shall indemnify the other against:

(a) Fines and penalties resulting from its breach

(b) Third-party claims arising from its breach

(c) Costs and expenses, including reasonable legal fees

**11.3 Limitation of Liability.**
