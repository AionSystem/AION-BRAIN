# Clinical Validators

Healthcare professionals who have validated AION-BRAIN's medical engines for accuracy, safety, and clinical applicability.

---

## Why Clinical Validation Matters

Medical AI carries unique risks. Clinical validators help ensure:

- **Accuracy** — Medical information is factually correct
- **Safety** — Patient safety principles are upheld
- **Applicability** — Frameworks work in real clinical contexts
- **Compliance** — Regulatory awareness is maintained

---

## Validation Criteria

### What We Validate

| Area | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Clinical Accuracy** | Medical facts, drug interactions, protocols |
| **Safety Protocols** | Patient harm prevention, escalation pathways |
| **Regulatory Awareness** | FDA, HIPAA, CMS compliance elements |
| **Workflow Integration** | Practical clinical applicability |
| **Edge Cases** | Rare conditions, complex presentations |

### Validator Qualifications

Validators should have:
- Active clinical license (MD, DO, NP, PA, RN, PharmD, etc.)
- Relevant specialty experience
- Understanding of AI limitations in healthcare
- Commitment to patient safety

## Validation Process

### How to Become a Clinical Validator

1. **Express Interest**
   - Email: AIONSYSTEM@outlook.com
   - Subject: "Clinical Validator Application"
   - Include: Credentials, specialty, interest area

2. **Credential Verification**
   - We verify active licensure
   - Confirm specialty expertise
   - Review conflict of interest

3. **Validation Assignment**
   - Receive specific engine components to review
   - Complete validation checklist
   - Provide written feedback

4. **Recognition**
   - Listed on this page
   - Credited in engine documentation
   - CME credit application (where applicable)

### Validation Checklist

Validators assess:

| Category | Questions |
|----------|-----------|
| **Accuracy** | Are medical facts correct? Are sources reliable? |
| **Completeness** | Are important considerations included? |
| **Safety** | Are safety warnings appropriate? Are red flags covered? |
| **Clarity** | Would a clinician understand the output? |
| **Limitations** | Are limitations clearly stated? |
