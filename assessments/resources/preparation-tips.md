## ðŸš« Common Mistakes to Avoid

### **1. Over-Complication**
- Simple, clear reasoning > complex jargon
- Depth matters more than breadth
- One well-developed idea beats three superficial ones

### **2. Defensiveness**
- Questions challenge you intentionally
- Show openness to being wrong
- Demonstrate learning orientation

### **3. Generic Answers**
- Avoid "on one hand, on the other" without depth
- Personal examples (when relevant) add authenticity
- Connect to your actual values and experiences

## ðŸ”„ Practice Exercises

### **To warm up:**
1. Take a complex problem you care about
2. Map out first, second, and third-order effects
3. Identify what you know, don't know, and can't know
4. Design a test to verify your assumptions

### **Paradox practice:**
- "If everyone were perfectly ethical, would ethics still exist?"
- "Does saving one life today that leads to ten deaths tomorrow count as saving a life?"
- "Can a system designed to prevent all failures itself become a failure mode?"
