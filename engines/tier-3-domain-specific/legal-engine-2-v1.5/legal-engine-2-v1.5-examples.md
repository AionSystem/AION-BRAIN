## EXAMPLE 4: Contract Risk Review

### Scenario
Reviewing $360K SaaS vendor agreement (3-year term, $120K/year) before signing.

### Key Findings

| Risk Area | Typical Vendor Position | Your Risk |
|-----------|------------------------|-----------|
| Data Ownership | Broad license to vendor | HIGH |
| Liability Cap | 12 months fees | MODERATE |
| SLA | Weak or none | MODERATE |
| Termination | No convenience right | HIGH |
| Data Exit | Not addressed | CRITICAL |

### Negotiation Priorities

| Priority | Items |
|----------|-------|
| P1 - Must Change | Data ownership, Data return, Liability cap, IP indemnity |
| P2 - Should Change | Termination right, SLA, Price cap, Mutual indemnity |
| P3 - Nice to Have | Transition assistance, Audit rights, Escrow |

### Exposure If Signed As-Is
- Worst case scenarios: $400K - $1.5M+
- Vendor failure, breach, price increase, inability to exit

### Attorney Consultation
- REQUIRED: Contract value >$100K
- REQUIRED: Indemnification/liability negotiation
- ESTIMATED BUDGET: $5,000 - $15,000

## EXAMPLE 5: Small Business Formation

### Scenario
Starting a consulting business in Texas, home-based, planning to hire 1-2 contractors.

### Key Findings

| Module | Output |
|--------|--------|
| Jurisdiction | Texas (state), IRS (federal), Local (city) |
| Recommended Entity | Single-Member LLC |
| Formation Cost | ~$300 (state filing) |
| Total Startup Cost | $800 - $4,000 |

### Formation Checklist

| Step | Status | Cost |
|------|--------|------|
| LLC Formation | Required | $300 |
| EIN | Required | Free |
| Operating Agreement | Recommended | $0-500 |
| Bank Account | Required | Free |
| Insurance (E&O) | Recommended | $500-2,000/yr |
| Contract Templates | Recommended | $0-1,500 |

### Texas Advantages
- No state income tax
- Franchise tax exemption if <$2.47M revenue
- Flexible contractor classification
- Online filing available

### Attorney Consultation
- RECOMMENDED: Operating agreement review
- RECOMMENDED: Contract template review
- ESTIMATED BUDGET: $500 - $2,000
