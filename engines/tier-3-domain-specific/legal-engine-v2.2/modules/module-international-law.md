# International Law Module (ILM-v1.0)

**Classification:** Legal Engine v2.2 Enhancement Module
**Purpose:** Cross-border legal analysis and international practice support

---

## 1. Module Overview

The International Law Module provides frameworks for analyzing cross-border transactions, international disputes, treaty interpretation, and multi-jurisdictional compliance.

### Core Functions

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Conflict of Laws | Choice of law analysis across jurisdictions |
| Treaty Analysis | International agreement interpretation |
| Cross-Border Transactions | Multi-jurisdiction deal structuring |
| International Dispute Resolution | Forum selection and enforcement |
| Regulatory Compliance | Multi-jurisdiction regulatory analysis |

## 2. Conflict of Laws Framework

### 2.1 Choice of Law Approaches

| Approach | Jurisdictions | Methodology |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| Territorial (1st Restatement) | Minority US states | Place of wrong/performance |
| Most Significant Relationship | Most US states | Section 6 factors analysis |
| Interest Analysis | CA, NJ, DC | Governmental interest weighing |
| Rome I/II | EU | Characteristic performance |
| Common Law | UK, Commonwealth | Proper law of contract |

## 4. International Dispute Resolution

### 4.1 Forum Selection Framework

| Forum Type | Advantages | Disadvantages | Best For |
|------------|------------|---------------|----------|
| Domestic Court | Familiar procedure, discovery | Enforcement challenges | Local disputes, injunctions |
| International Arbitration | Neutral, enforceable | Cost, limited discovery | Commercial disputes |
| ICSID | Specialized, sovereign disputes | Limited to treaty claims | Investment disputes |
| WTO DSB | Binding on states | State-to-state only | Trade disputes |
| ICJ | Authoritative | Consent required | State disputes |

## 7. Cultural and Practice Considerations

### 7.1 Legal System Differences

| Common Law | Civil Law | Considerations |
|------------|-----------|----------------|
| Precedent-based | Code-based | Interpretation approach |
| Adversarial | Inquisitorial | Procedure expectations |
| Discovery | Limited disclosure | Document production |
| Jury trials | Judge trials | Presentation style |
| Oral advocacy | Written submissions | Emphasis differs |

### 7.2 Practice Variations

| Jurisdiction | Key Practice Differences |
|--------------|-------------------------|
| US | Extensive discovery, contingency fees, jury trials |
| UK | Loser pays, barristers/solicitors, limited discovery |
| Germany | Civil law, specialized courts, document-heavy |
| France | Civil law, investigating judge, state influence |
| China | State influence, enforcement challenges, relationship focus |
| Japan | Relationship-based, mediation preference, slow courts |
